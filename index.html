How the peg pyramid works

The board is built as a triangle of staggered pegs, with each lower row one peg wider than the row above it. A ball that starts at the top has a fifty-fifty chance of moving left or right at every step, and that pattern repeats until it reaches the bottom. The result is one pocket from the line across the base — always one more pocket than the number of rows.

Because each bounce is a coin-flip style turn, most balls collect near the centre, while outer pockets are much rarer. In maths terms, the layout acts like a Galton board and produces a binomial curve. The payout table simply flips that curve: common middle pockets pay less, while rare edge pockets pay more.
